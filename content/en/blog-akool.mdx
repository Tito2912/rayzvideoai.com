<h2 id="usages-title">Use Cases</h2>
<ul class="bullets">
<li><strong>Localization</strong> of a video into multiple languages while maintaining consistent <em>lip movements</em> (lip-sync), for tutorials, onboarding, support. </li>
<li><strong>AI Presenter</strong>: talking avatar for product announcements, customer messages, landing pages, animated FAQs.</li>
<li><strong>Face swap</strong> control: marketing demos, prototypes, and creative concepts <em>with rights respect</em>.</li>
</ul>

<section aria-labelledby="modules-title" class="intro" id="modules">
<div class="container">
<h2 id="modules-title">Akool Modules: Strengths &amp; Limitations</h2>
<h3>Talking Photo / Talking Avatar</h3>
<ul class="bullets">
<li>Animates a photo (or avatar) from existing text/voice. </li>
<li>Quality linked to source photo (lighting, sharpness, framing) and provided audio.</li>
</ul>
<h3>LipSync &amp; Video Translate</h3>
<ul class="bullets">
<li>Synchronizes lips with a <strong>target audio</strong> for credible rendering. </li>
<li>Ideal for multilingual <em>dubbing</em>; check rights and consents.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Face Swap</h3>
<ul class="bullets">
<li>Face substitution on photo/video (ethical use required). </li>
</ul>
<h3>Pricing &amp; Credits</h3>
<p>Akool works with <strong>tiers/subscriptions</strong> and credits per modules. Refer to the official page for current pricing grid. </p>
</div>
</section>
<section aria-labelledby="workflow-title" class="intro" id="workflow">
<div class="container">
<h2 id="workflow-title">Step-by-Step Workflows</h2>
<h3>A) Translate a Video with Lip-sync</h3>
<ol class="bullets">
<li>Prepare your <strong>sources</strong>: original video, translated script, target audio file (or TTS).</li>
<li>In Akool, open <em>Video Translate / LipSync</em>, import video and target audio.</li>
<li>Adjust options (language, movement intensity, timing) and start rendering.</li>
<li>Check <strong>pronunciation of key words</strong> (names, brands), rerun if needed.</li>
</ol>
<h3>B) Create a "Talking Photo"</h3>
<ol class="bullets">
<li>Select a sharp photo (head well framed, good lighting).</li>
<li>Write a short text (≤ 120 words) or provide quality audio (16 kHz recommended by some integrators). </li>
<li>Preview, adjust intonation, export.</li>
</ol>
<h3>C) Responsible Face Swap</h3>
<ul class="bullets">
<li>Obtain necessary <strong>authorizations</strong> (image rights) and respect TOS.</li>
<li>Use stable source videos, well lit, with clearly visible faces.</li>
</ul>

<section aria-labelledby="api-title" class="intro" id="api">
<div class="container">
<h2 id="api-title">API &amp; Automation</h2>
<p>Akool exposes <strong>open APIs</strong> (Talking Photo, Talking Avatar, VoiceLab, LipSync, etc.) useful for integrating talking avatars, translation and voice into your pipelines. Note that <em>resources generated by the API expire after 7 days</em> (save your links/assets). </p>
</div>

<section aria-labelledby="comparaisons-title" class="intro" id="comparaisons">
<div class="container">
<h2 id="comparaisons-title">Comparisons</h2>
<ul class="bullets">
<li><strong>vs HeyGen</strong>: HeyGen offers a very accessible "presenter" studio; Akool stands out with its <em>precise lip-sync/translation modules</em> and API approach. </li>
<li><strong>vs Pictory</strong>: Pictory excels at generating video from text; Akool is tailored for <em>localization</em> and synthesis/speech. </li>
<li><strong>vs InVideo</strong>: InVideo focuses on <em>templates &amp; fast editing</em> for social ads; Akool is more oriented towards avatars/lip-sync. </li>
</ul>
