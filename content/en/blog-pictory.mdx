<section aria-labelledby="usages-title" class="intro" id="usages">
<div class="container">
<h2 id="usages-title">Relevant Use Cases</h2>
<p>Pictory is ideal when you start from <strong>text content</strong> (script, article, notes, transcript) and need to output a <strong>first video draft</strong> quickly. Three recurring scenarios:</p>
<ul class="bullets">
<li><strong>Article Recycling</strong>: transform your blog posts into YouTube/LinkedIn capsules. Automatic segmentation into scenes already sets a visual framework.</li>
<li><strong>Video Newsletters</strong>: summarize long text into short format with auto subtitles, perfect for Shorts/Reels.</li>
<li><strong>Courses &amp; E-learning</strong>: generate standardized educational sequences (intro, key points, recap), to enrich later with your own assets.</li>
</ul>
<p>Pictory's promise is <strong>speed</strong>: produce a draft <em>in minutes</em> instead of hours. The AI engine suggests visuals/media and generates subtitles. You keep control over scenes, cuts, titles and music to finalize.</p>
</div>
</section>
<section aria-labelledby="forces-title" class="intro" id="forces">
<div class="container">
<h2 id="forces-title">Strengths &amp; Limitations</h2>
<h3>Strengths</h3>
<ul class="bullets">
<li><strong>Script/text → video</strong> very fast thanks to models and AI assistant (segmentation, visuals, subtitles). </li>
<li><strong>Templates</strong> and <strong>stock</strong> included depending on chosen plan (Storyblocks / Getty). </li>
<li><strong>AI Voices</strong> standard multilingual + ElevenLabs voice minutes in Pro plan (according to pricing page). </li>
</ul>
<h3>Limitations to Know</h3>
<ul class="bullets">
<li>No realistic native <strong>AI avatar</strong> (for this, prefer HeyGen / Akool).</li>
<li>Without <strong>customization</strong> (brand kit, B-rolls), rendering may seem generic.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</section>
<section aria-labelledby="workflow-title" class="intro" id="workflow">
<div class="container">
<h2 id="workflow-title">Step-by-Step Workflow (from idea to render)</h2>
<h3>1) Prepare Your Material</h3>
<ul class="bullets">
<li>Choose a source: structured <em>script</em>, article <em>URL</em>, cleaned <em>transcript</em>.</li>
<li>Structure into <strong>sections of 1–3 sentences</strong> (favors one scene = one idea).</li>
<li>Gather your <strong>brand assets</strong>: HEX colors, logo, fonts, outro, B-rolls.</li>
</ul>
<h3>2) Import &amp; Let AI Propose</h3>
<p>In Pictory, paste your text/URL then let AI <em>segment into scenes</em> and <em>suggest media</em>. Select a template, adjust scene duration and title hierarchy.</p>
<h3>3) Customize &amp; Refine</h3>
<ul class="bullets">
<li>Activate your <strong>brand kit</strong>: palette, fonts, title styles, outro.</li>
<li>Replace media with your B-rolls if needed to avoid too "stock" effect.</li>
<li>Check subtitle <strong>readability</strong> (size, contrast, position).</li>
<li>Work on opening <strong>hook</strong> (3–5 s) and conclude with clear CTA.</li>
</ul>
<h3>4) Voiceover &amp; Music</h3>
<p>Choose a standard AI voice (or use your ElevenLabs minutes if your plan allows), adjust <em>timing</em> and <em>auto-sync</em> of voice to editing. </p>
<h3>5) Export &amp; Variations</h3>
<p>Export in 16:9 then generate vertical versions for Shorts/Reels. Publish, measure retention and iterate.</p>

<section aria-labelledby="checklists-title" class="intro" id="checklists">
<div class="container">
<h2 id="checklists-title">Practical Checklists</h2>
<h3>Before Generating</h3>
<ul class="bullets">
<li>Script divided into short blocks (idea/scene).</li>
<li>Brand kit ready (colors, fonts, logo, outro).</li>
<li>Internal B-rolls available (avoids 100% stock).</li>
</ul>
<h3>To Check During Editing</h3>
<ul class="bullets">
<li>Title hierarchy (H1/H2/H3) and rhythm (changes every 3–6 s).</li>
<li>Subtitle readability (contrast &amp; size), safe areas.</li>
<li>Hook &amp; CTA: clear promise at opening, clear action at end.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Before Export</h3>
<ul class="bullets">
<li>Adapted formats (16:9 YouTube, 9:16 Shorts/Reels/TikTok).</li>
<li>Audio check (peak -1 dB, clear voice, music -18 to -12 LUFS).</li>
<li>Filename &amp; chapters (YouTube Chapters if long-form).</li>
</ul>
</div>
</section>
<section aria-labelledby="integrations-title" class="intro" id="integrations">
<div class="container">
<h2 id="integrations-title">Integrations &amp; Exports</h2>
<p>Pictory offers a "studio" experience focused on speed: AI generation, stock library according to plan, auto subtitles and voice synchronization. Quotas, media rights and access to Getty/Storyblocks depend on the <strong>chosen offer</strong> (Starter/Professional/Team). </p>
</div>
</section>
<section aria-labelledby="comparaisons-title" class="intro" id="comparaisons">
<div class="container">
<h2 id="comparaisons-title">Quick Comparisons</h2>
<ul class="bullets">
<li><strong>vs HeyGen</strong>: HeyGen is centered on <em>presenter avatar</em> &amp; slides studio; Pictory excels at transforming text into edited sequences. </li>
<li><strong>vs Akool</strong>: Akool shines on <em>video translation/lip-sync</em> and modular avatars; Pictory serves "script → video" better. </li>
<li><strong>vs InVideo</strong>: InVideo focuses on <em>large template library</em> and express editing for marketing; Pictory mainly automates from text. </li>
</ul>
</div>
