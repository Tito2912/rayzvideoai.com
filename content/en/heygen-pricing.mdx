# HeyGen pricing (2026)

HeyGen is popular for **avatar presenter videos** and **video translation/dubbing** with lip-sync. The right plan depends mostly on your **volume**, **average length**, and how many **languages** you need to ship.

## 1) What actually drives cost

Before looking at a “$/month” label, identify:

- **videos/month** (or minutes/quotas depending on plan)
- **average length** (30–60s vs 3–5 min)
- **languages** (if you do dubbing/translation)
- export quality (HD/4K depending on plan)
- collaboration (seats, roles, approvals)
- API / automation (if you scale production)

## 2) Plans: how to read them (Free → Pro/Team)

Typical structure:

- **Free**: test workflow and quality, with quotas and limits.
- **Creator/Pro**: steady solo production (more volume, better options).
- **Team/Business**: collaboration + higher volume.
- **Enterprise**: specific needs (security, SLA, support, custom volume).

The best indicator is not the plan name: it’s whether the **real limits** fit your workflow.

## 3) Simple budget estimation method

1. List your formats: “60s avatar”, “3-min demo”, “dubbing into 3 languages”, etc.
2. Estimate monthly volume per format.
3. Add margin for iterations, A/B tests, retakes.

Quick example: 20 videos/month × 1 min + 2 translated variants = ~60 “units” to produce (order of magnitude). Then match against plan capacity.

## 4) Reduce cost without losing quality

Common optimizations:

- reuse templates (intro/outro, lower thirds, backgrounds)
- standardize scripts (length, structure, CTA)
- limit random iterations (1–2 review loops, not 10)
- translate only top-performing videos first
- keep brand assets consistent to avoid retakes
