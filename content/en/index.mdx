<h2 id="intro-title">Video creation powered by AI: simplify, accelerate, scale</h2>
<p>Video has become the king channel to capture attention, educate and convert. The problem: producing quality videos regularly requires time, skills and budget. AI video tools change the game: they automate heavy tasks (writing, storyboarding, editing, subtitling, localization), reduce costs and allow teams and solopreneurs to go from idea to final render in record time.</p>
<p>On this page, you'll find a clear comparison, practical sheets and official links to start right away. We cover four major needs: <strong>script → video</strong> (Pictory: brand kits, highlights, 9:16/1:1 exports), <strong>talking avatars &amp; localization</strong> (HeyGen: avatars + dubbing/translation; Akool: video translation + lip-sync), and <strong>fast template-based editing</strong> (InVideo: templates, iStock, HD exports). Each tool has its strengths and limitations — the key is to assemble <em>your</em> ideal workflow.</p>
</div>

<h3 id="pictory-title">Pictory — Script/URL → Video in no time</h3>
<p><strong>Positioning:</strong> turn text (script, article, web page) into an edited video with scenes, media and subtitles. Key points (checked 03/01/2026): <strong>brand kits</strong>, <strong>9:16</strong>/<strong>1:1</strong> exports, <strong>video highlights</strong> and <strong>ElevenLabs</strong> AI voice minutes depending on plan.</p>
<h4>Strengths</h4>
<ul class="bullets">
<li>Generates a <strong>first draft</strong> quickly (Script/URL → scenes + captions).</li>
<li><strong>Brand kits</strong> to reuse logo, colors, fonts and styles (plan-dependent).</li>
<li><strong>ElevenLabs</strong> AI voice minutes + standard voices (plan-dependent).</li>
<li><strong>Highlights</strong> + portrait <strong>9:16</strong> and square <strong>1:1</strong> exports.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Limits to know</h4>
<ul class="bullets">
<li>Less suitable for realistic "presenter" videos (no native avatar).</li>
<li>Style may seem generic if you don't <strong>customize</strong> templates and brand kit.</li>
</ul>
<h4>Typical workflow (3 steps)</h4>
<ol class="bullets">
<li>Paste your script/URL → let Pictory segment into scenes.</li>
<li>Apply your <strong>brand kit</strong>, swap key media, check titles/subtitles.</li>
<li>Export in 16:9, then create 9:16/1:1 versions and highlights if needed.</li>
</ol>

<div class="container">
<h2 id="guide-title">Which tool to choose? Build your winning workflow</h2>
<p>There is no universal "best" AI video tool. The right choice depends on your format, publication pace and resources. Here are three quick paths to start:</p>
<div class="guide-grid">
<div class="guide-card">
<h3>1) Script → Recurrent video</h3>
<p>You already have a text flow (articles, scripts). Use <strong>Pictory</strong> to generate a draft, refine editing, add your <strong>brand kit</strong> (colors, typography, outro), and publish on YouTube + Shorts/Reels variations.</p>
</div>
<div class="guide-card">
<h3>2) Multilingual presenter avatar</h3>
<p>You want a virtual presenter without filming. Start with <strong>HeyGen</strong>, then if needed translate/localize with <strong>Akool</strong> (lipsync/video translation). Perfect for tutorialization, onboarding, product announcements.</p>
</div>
<div class="guide-card">
<h3>3) Social ads &amp; promotions</h3>
<p>You need to produce promotional videos quickly and often. Rely on <strong>InVideo</strong> (templates + quick tools), create A/B variants, and recycle the best ones into vertical formats to capture attention.</p>
</div>
</div>
<h3>Decision checklist</h3>
<ul class="bullets">
<li><strong>Target formats:</strong> shorts, long-form, courses, ads?</li>
<li><strong>Monthly volume:</strong> how many videos and on which channels?</li>
<li><strong>Languages:</strong> need for talking avatars, voiceover, multilingual subtitles?</li>
<li><strong>Customization:</strong> "speedrun" templates vs demanding brand kit.</li>
<li><strong>Integrations:</strong> YouTube, Drive, DAM, marketing tools.</li>
<li><strong>Budget:</strong> fixed subscription vs credits per action.</li>
</ul>
</div>
