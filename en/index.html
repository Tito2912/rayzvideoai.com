Video creation powered by AI: simplify, accelerate, scale

Video has become the king channel to capture attention, educate and convert. The problem: producing quality videos regularly requires time, skills and budget. AI video tools change the game: they automate heavy tasks (writing, storyboarding, editing, subtitling, localization), reduce costs and allow teams and solopreneurs to go from idea to final render in record time.

On this page, you'll find a clear comparison, practical sheets and official links to start right away. We cover four major needs: script → video (Pictory with brand kit 2.0, highlights, 9:16 exports), talking avatars & video translation (Akool real-time lip-sync, HeyGen Instant/Photo Avatar + Video Translate 4.0), and fast template-based editing (InVideo with brand kit, iStock, 9:16 resize). Each tool has its strengths and limitations - the important thing is to assemble your ideal workflow.

Why use AI video in 2025?

  • Speed: go from idea to first draft in minutes instead of hours/days.
  • Cost: reduce filming/editing expenses on repetitive formats.
  • Scalability: produce consistent video series for social media, onboarding or training.
  • Localization: generate multilingual voiceover, subtitles and lipsync to reach more markets.
  • Accessibility: democratize video for independents, SMEs and marketing teams.

Result: more editorial consistency, better presence on key platforms (YouTube, Shorts/Reels/TikTok, LinkedIn) and improving ROI thanks to publication frequency.

The 4 selected tools: strengths, limits and use cases

Each solution addresses a specific need. The goal is not to "do everything with one tool", but to assemble the perfect duo/trio for your context.

Pictory — Script/URL → Video in no time

Positioning: transform text (script, article, web page) into edited video with scenes, media and subtitles. 2025 updates: brand kit 2.0, ElevenLabs minutes in Pro, highlights and multi-format exports (16:9 → 9:16/1:1).

Strengths

  • Generates a first draft fast (Story/Article→video) with auto scenes/subtitles.
  • Brand kit 2.0: palettes, fonts, lower thirds, intros/outros as presets.
  • ElevenLabs minutes (Pro) for premium multilingual voices.
  • Highlights extraction + smart crop for vertical outputs.

Limits to know

  • Less suitable for realistic "presenter" videos (no native avatar).
  • Style may seem generic if you don't customize templates and brand kit.

Typical workflow (3 steps)

  1. Paste your script/URL → let Pictory segment into scenes (Story/Article).
  2. Apply your brand kit, swap key media, check titles/subtitles.
  3. Generate, resize to 9:16 with smart crop, export and repurpose highlights.

Akool — Talking avatars, lip-sync & video translation

Positioning: create talking avatars, translate videos with real-time lip-sync and quickly localize content. Perfect for onboarding, e-learning, customer support and multi-language marketing.

Strengths

  • 2025 modules: Talking Photo 2.0, real-time LipSync, smoother Video Translate.
  • Face swap HD cleaner on edges/hair (use with consent).
  • API/SDK with webhooks to automate (chatbot, internal app).
  • Good complement to HeyGen if you want to refine localization (lip-sync).

Limits to know

  • Credits per action: plan your volume to control costs.
  • Final quality depends on provided assets (photo/video/audio source).

Typical workflow (3 steps)

  1. Load source portrait/video + target text/voice (or TTS audio).
  2. Choose module (Talking, LipSync, Translate) and tune intensity/tempo.
  3. Preview, adjust timing/lip-sync, export and integrate into your edit.

HeyGen — Videos with virtual presenter

Positioning: produce videos with a presenter avatar (your generated face or a library avatar) for courses, onboarding, product announcements, internal updates and tutorials. 2025 updates: Instant Avatar, Photo Avatar, Video Translate 4.0, Streaming Avatar/API.

Strengths

  • Instant/Photo Avatar to create a personal avatar in minutes.
  • Video Translate 4.0: better dubbing/lip-sync across more languages.
  • Streaming Avatar + API: live demos, bots or interactive onboarding.
  • Integrated editing + prompt-to-video generation for some cases.

Limits to know

  • "Studio" rendering: ideal for educational & corporate, less "cinematic".
  • Advanced customization (brand, assets) requires some time.

Typical workflow (3 steps)

  1. Choose avatar (Instant/Photo or library), paste your script.
  2. Adjust slides/backgrounds, add screenshots and captions.
  3. Generate, review, export in 16:9 or 9:16, then localize if needed.

InVideo — Templates & fast editing

Positioning: an editor designed to go fast with a huge library of templates and assets, plus an improved InVideo AI (prompt→script→video). 2025 updates: brand kit, iStock (paid plans), 9:16 resize and voice/clone.

Strengths

  • Range of templates for ads, social media, presentations, trailers.
  • Brand kit + iStock to standardize campaigns.
  • Resize 16:9 → 9:16/1:1 with smart repositioning.
  • AI voices + voice clone for more personalized outputs.

Limits to know

  • Rendering can be "template-like" if you don't edit enough.
  • Advanced AI options vary by plan and included minutes.

Typical workflow (3 steps)

  1. Choose template adapted to format (9:16, 1:1, 16:9) or an AI prompt.
  2. Apply your brand kit, replace text/media, add voice/clone.
  3. Resize to 9:16, add opening hook, check timing, export.

Which tool to choose? Build your winning workflow

There is no universal "best" AI video tool. The right choice depends on your format, publication pace and resources. Here are three quick paths to start:

1) Script → Recurrent video

You already have a text flow (articles, scripts). Use Pictory to generate a draft, refine editing, add your brand kit (colors, typography, outro), and publish on YouTube + Shorts/Reels variations.

2) Multilingual presenter avatar

You want a virtual presenter without filming. Start with HeyGen, then if needed translate/localize with Akool (lipsync/video translation). Perfect for tutorialization, onboarding, product announcements.

3) Social ads & promotions

You need to produce promotional videos quickly and often. Rely on InVideo (templates + quick tools), create A/B variants, and recycle the best ones into vertical formats to capture attention.

Decision checklist

  • Target formats: shorts, long-form, courses, ads?
  • Monthly volume: how many videos and on which channels?
  • Languages: need for talking avatars, voiceover, multilingual subtitles?
  • Customization: "speedrun" templates vs demanding brand kit.
  • Integrations: YouTube, Drive, DAM, marketing tools.
  • Budget: fixed subscription vs credits per action.
